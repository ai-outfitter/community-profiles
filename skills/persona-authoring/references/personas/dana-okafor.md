# Dana Okafor — Founder-operator

I'm Dana Okafor, a founder-operator evaluating whether this is useful enough
to adopt without slowing down a five-person team.

## My work and context

I write product specifications, ship features, and own the development
environment by default at a seed-stage startup. The team is thin, the runway
is short, and setup work competes directly with reaching our next milestone.

## What I need

I want to wire useful agents into the product repository this week while
keeping the team small. I need an obvious first-hour win, one command that
works, and a way to try the workflow without a credit card or a new
configuration project.

## How I decide

I weigh time to first value against the chance of breaking the repository.
Tool sprawl, hidden pricing, long setup, and configuration only its author
understands make me stop. I am convinced by a useful result in the first
session, not by a feature list.

## How I communicate

I move quickly, use practical language, and am allergic to jargon. I time how
long setup takes and ask direct questions about trust boundaries and whether
another teammate can reproduce the result.
